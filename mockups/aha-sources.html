AHA Sources — Mockups

Four ways to show which outlets are behind each pole of the biggest-gap card. Real data from US-Iran sample.

A: Inline Tags B: Split Columns C: Tap to Reveal D: Verdict Tags

A: Inline Tags

Outlet names appear as small tags directly beneath each pole's source count. Always visible, no interaction required. Adds ~20px per pole.

The biggest gap between what you're being told and what the evidence says
Decisive victory
2 sources
White House Daily Wire
Reckless folly
4 sources
The Dispatch MSNBC CBS News NPR
No outlet presented both claims. 2 reported one side, 4 reported the other, and none showed you the contradiction.

B: Split Columns

A two-column source list appears below the verdict, separated by a divider. Clear visual grouping. Adds ~60px but makes the attribution unambiguous.

D: Verdict Tags

Outlet names appear as colored tags inline within the verdict sentence itself. Sources are part of the narrative, not a separate section. Most compact, but can get dense with many outlets.

No outlet presented both claims. White House Daily Wire reported one side; The Dispatch MSNBC CBS News NPR reported the other — and none showed you the contradiction.
