How we know this

Every number on this page was generated by an AI pipeline. Here's how, and where it could be wrong.

Pipeline

13 articles scraped → AI extracted claims blindly (outlet hidden) → claims cross-referenced across outlets → opinion axes identified → sourcing quality estimated

  • Claim extraction — the AI decides what counts as a "claim." A different model might extract differently.
  • Sourcing quality (0.0–1.0) — LLM-estimated, not computed from a validated rubric. Treat as rough tiers, not precise measurements.
  • Axis selection — which disagreements are surfaced is itself an editorial choice.
  • Bias labels — from AllSides, used for display ordering only, not as analytical input.

What this tool cannot tell you

This covers 13 English-language US sources from one day. It cannot detect stories no outlet covered, verify classified intelligence, or account for non-English coverage.

Organizing information into "left vs. right" is itself a political framework that structurally rewards centrist positions. This tool has a bias toward the center — not because centrism is correct, but because its methodology rewards cross-spectrum agreement.
