How this analysis was made

1
Article collection: 13 articles from outlets spanning left, center, and right. Outlet selection is an editorial choice that affects results.
2
Claim extraction: An AI model (Claude) read each article and extracted factual claims. The model decides what counts as a "claim" — a different model might extract differently.
3
Sourcing classification: For each claim-outlet pair, the AI classified the sourcing type. These categories are relatively objective but edge cases exist.
4
Opinion axis identification: The AI identified 18 questions where outlets took opposing positions. Which questions get identified is itself an editorial act.
5
Sourcing quality scores (0.0-1.0): For each opinion, the AI estimated how well the outlet supported its position. This is the most subjective step. Treat these as rough tiers, not precise measurements.
Known limitations: This tool uses an AI model with its own training biases. It may systematically favor certain framings, underweight non-Western perspectives, or apply sourcing standards inconsistently. Cross-spectrum agreement can reflect shared assumptions rather than truth. This is a starting point for critical reading, not a replacement for it.
