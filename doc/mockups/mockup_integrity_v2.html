How this analysis was made

This page was generated by a four-phase automated pipeline. Every number below was produced by an LLM (Claude), not by human analysts or verified algorithms. This matters because:

  • Bias classifications (left/right/center) are assigned by the LLM based on AllSides ratings and its own training data. These assignments are themselves editorial acts.
  • Sourcing quality scores (0.0-1.0) are LLM-estimated, not computed from a validated rubric. Different runs may produce different scores.
  • Claim extraction decides what counts as a "fact" vs. "opinion" -- a judgment call the LLM makes, not an objective distinction.
  • Axis selection determines which disagreements are surfaced. Different axis choices would tell a different story about the same coverage.

The pipeline is deterministic given the same inputs, but the inputs themselves (which articles were scraped, how text was chunked) introduce variability. Treat all numbers as approximate indicators, not measurements.

Editorial choices this app made (that a neutral tool shouldn't hide)

  • We selected 3 of 18 axes to display below. We chose axes with the widest sourcing disparity between poles. A different selection criterion would surface different disagreements.
  • Outlet positions on the left-right spectrum use AllSides ratings as a starting point, but the LLM adjusts positions per-claim.
  • The "consensus" section shows claims where many outlets agree. Agreement does not equal truth -- all outlets relied on the same primary sources for most "consensus" facts.
  • This tool does not cover what was NOT reported. Omissions are invisible in this data.

What this tool cannot tell you

This analysis covers 13 English-language US sources scraped on one day. It cannot detect stories no outlet covered. It cannot verify classified intelligence. It cannot account for non-English coverage.

The act of organizing information into "left vs. right" is itself a political framework that advantages centrist positions. This tool has a bias toward the center -- not because centrism is correct, but because its methodology structurally rewards cross-spectrum agreement.
