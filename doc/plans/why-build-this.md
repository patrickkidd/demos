# "Why Build This?" Page — Theoretical Foundation

## Core Premise

News outlets are locked in reactive triangles with each other. Left-leaning
and right-leaning outlets aren't independently reporting — they're responding
to each other's framing, emphasis, and omissions. This emotional reactivity
(Bowen's triangle concept) distorts each individual outlet's view of events.

The irony: this very reactivity is what creates the viewport diversity required
for collective intelligence. Because outlets are mutually reactive, they cover
different angles, emphasize different facts, and surface different evidence.
No single outlet covers more than ~65% of the verifiable claims in a story.

By aggregating the reactive plurality into a single centroid view — and by
showing what any isolated source leaves out — this tool helps people
differentiate from the societal emotional process and form their own
evidence-based understanding.

## Bowen Theory Connection

This app is a direct application of Murray Bowen's eighth concept: **Societal
Emotional Process**. Bowen observed that the same emotional dynamics that
operate in families operate in society at large:

- **Triangles in society**: Media outlets are locked in the same kind of
  interlocking triangles Bowen described in families. Outlet A reacts to
  Outlet B's framing. Outlet C responds to the A-B dynamic. The reader who
  consumes only one outlet is unknowingly inside a triangle, receiving a
  reactive position as though it were objective reporting.

- **Functional level of differentiation**: An outlet's coverage on a given day
  reflects its functional level of differentiation — how much it can report
  facts vs. how much it's driven by the emotional process of the news cycle.
  Under stress (crisis, elections, conflict), functional differentiation drops
  and outlets become more reactive, more polarized, more predictable.

- **Pseudo-self vs. solid self**: Each outlet's daily coverage is largely
  pseudo-self — positions borrowed from its reactive relationships with other
  outlets and its audience. The centroid view this tool produces is closer to
  the "solid self" of the story: "clearly defined beliefs, opinions,
  convictions" based on evidence rather than emotional reactivity.

- **The detriangled third**: Bowen's core therapeutic insight is that "the
  emotional problem between two people will resolve automatically if they
  remain in contact with a third person who can remain free of the emotional
  field between them, while actively relating to each." This tool IS the
  detriangled third — in active contact with both poles of the media triangle
  without getting pulled into either side's emotional field.

## What the Tool Actually Does

For each story on a given day:

1. **Collects** articles from 20+ outlets across the political spectrum
2. **Extracts claims blindly** — the AI doesn't know which outlet wrote
   each article during extraction, preventing preconceived bias
3. **Cross-references** factual claims across outlets to find:
   - **Consensus facts**: claims reported by 5+ outlets across the spectrum
   - **One-sided facts**: claims only covered by left or right
   - **Opinion axes**: the specific questions where outlets take opposing
     positions, with sourcing quality scored for each pole
4. **Synthesizes** a centroid view that weights evidence quality over volume
5. **Shows blind spots**: what you miss by reading only your preferred source

## The "Why" for the Average Reader

The page aimed at a general audience (IQ 100, no Bowen background) should
communicate:

### Problem statement
- "You're not getting the full story from any single source"
- Not because of conspiracy or malice, but because news outlets are locked in
  a pattern of mutual reaction that structurally limits what each one covers
- Show a concrete example: "7 of 12 outlets reported X. If you only read
  [outlet], you never saw it."

### What this tool does differently
- It reads ALL the sources so you don't have to
- It extracts facts without knowing which outlet wrote them (blind extraction)
- It shows you exactly what your source covered and what it missed
- It doesn't tell you what to think — it shows you what the evidence says
  across all sources, weighted by sourcing quality

### The honest caveat
- This tool has a structural bias toward the center — not because centrism is
  correct, but because its methodology rewards cross-spectrum agreement
- AI extraction is imperfect — a different model might extract differently
- Sourcing quality scores are estimates, not ground truth
- "Organizing information into left vs. right is itself a political framework"

### The Bowen connection (for those who want to go deeper)
- Brief accessible explanation: "The same patterns that family therapists
  observe in anxious families — people reacting to each other rather than
  thinking clearly — operate in how news organizations cover stories"

## Naming Candidates from Bowen's Language

These emerged from reading FTiCP for terms that map to the tool's function:

- **Solid Self** — the centroid view as the story's solid self vs. each
  outlet's pseudo-self position
- **Detriangle** — Bowen's coined verb for what the tool does to the reader
- **Functional Fact** — Bowen's distinction: what happened vs. why (editorial
  spin). The tool extracts functional facts.
- **The Calm Third** — derived from the detriangled third principle. The tool
  is the party that stays in active contact with both poles without joining
  either side's emotional field.
- **Outside Position** — "In periods of stress, the outside position is the
  most comfortable and most desired position." The tool offers the outside
  position on the media triangle.
- **Wider Lens** — "it was hoped that the broad assumptions might help
  observers see through a wider lens" (p.463)
- **Nodal** — "Each person becomes a nodal point or an electronic center
  through which impulses pass" (p.467). Each outlet is a nodal point.
