# News Reader - Why This Exists

## You're not getting the full story

Not because of conspiracy or malice — because of how the news industry works.

News outlets react to each other. Left-leaning and right-leaning outlets
aren't independently reporting — they're responding to each other's framing,
emphasis, and omissions. Each one covers a different slice of reality.

The result: no single source covers more than about 65% of the verifiable
claims in a given story.

## What this tool does

For each story it tracks, the News Reader collects articles from 20+ outlets
across the political spectrum, then:

1. **Extracts claims blindly** — the AI doesn't know which outlet wrote each
   article during extraction, preventing bias from shaping what gets pulled out
2. **Cross-references** claims across all outlets to find:
   - **Consensus facts** — reported by 5+ outlets across the spectrum
   - **One-sided coverage** — facts only reported by left or right
   - **Opinion axes** — questions where outlets disagree, with sourcing quality
     scored for each side
3. **Shows your blind spots** — pick any outlet and see exactly what it covered
   and what it missed

It doesn't tell you what to think. It shows what the evidence says across all
sources, weighted by sourcing quality.

## Honest caveats

- **Center bias** — the methodology rewards cross-spectrum agreement. If the
  truth falls on one side, this tool will still pull toward the center.
- **AI extraction is imperfect** — a different model might extract differently.
- **Sourcing scores are estimates**, not ground truth.
- **Left vs. right is a simplification** that obscures as much as it reveals.

## The deeper theory

The patterns this tool addresses are the same ones family therapists have
studied for decades. Murray Bowen observed that in anxious families, people
react to each other rather than thinking independently — and that these
dynamics operate at the societal level too.

News outlets are locked in what Bowen called *triangles* — reactive
relationships where each party's position is shaped more by the others than by
independent assessment. A reader consuming one outlet is inside this triangle,
receiving a reactive position as though it were objective reporting.

This tool attempts to be the *detriangled third* — in contact with both sides
without joining either side's emotional field.
